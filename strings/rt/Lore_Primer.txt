<p><strong><em><span style="font-size:115%"> Welcome to Domotan Island! </span></em></strong></p>
<p> This lore primer serves as an introduction to Domotan Island's lore and world. <br>
This is a reflection of the initial summary of the Universe page on our wiki. That page is practically mandatory reading, but you can use this for quick reference. Not all information is available here, and some secrets still lie within item descriptions, inscriptions, or booklets- it's a primer, not a compendium. </p>
<hr>
<strong><span style="font-size:105%"> Some quick vocabulary/concepts important to the setting: </span></strong>
<p><strong> The Goblet of Life </strong> - The area of the Earth which Domotan Island resides within, a small crater-like cup that represents a small portion of the ancient world. </p>
<p><strong> Domotan </strong> - The island that the game takes place on, one of the most isolated areas placed precariously in the center of the Goblet-spanning ocean called the Sea of Fire. </p>
<p><strong> Elementals </strong> - The divines that rule the world, now. These are Gods fueled by naught but primal emotion, and their power is linked directly to however many might worship them. </p>
<p><strong> The Jealous God </strong> - The pseudo-divine that lords over Domotan Island, a deity which empowers the royal family and serves as its only true God. </p>

	<summary><strong><span style="font-size:130%"> BASICS OF THE GOBLET </span></strong></summary>

	<p><strong>  The World at Large </strong><em> - "The Goblet of Life as it is today was made by an apocalyptic event many years ago that resulted in Hell piercing the firmament of the Earth, rendering the majority of the planet a sundered wasteland. It is a much smaller place, now, and in the continental circumference of the Goblet resides the bastardized facsimiles of whatever lost kingdoms once fled here. In the protective arms of the Elementals, the Goblet was sealed, and in the thousand-some-years that passed, six survivors yet stand."  </em>
	<br></p>

	<p><strong>  The Faces of Power </strong><em> - "In a ring around the vast Sea of Fire, these six enduring empires stand in testament to forgotten vows that their ancestors once made to the Elementals. Each represents a facet of their respective God, which in turn embodies the most commanding passion in their people's history."  </em>
	<br></p>

	<p><strong>  The Motonium and the Katholikos </strong><em> - "The Elementals gather power and sway through means of worship, and thus the balance between the deities is the most sacred of pursuits. The Elemental-hearing world thus declares its theological allegiance to a powerful organization called the Katholikos, whose prosecuting voice is the judge of all who might seek to disrupt the teetering equilibrium of divine favor."  </em>
	<br></p>

	<p><strong>  The Heretics </strong><em> - "Favoring any one deity that is not Elemental is heresy, if only in that it might seek to upset the Katholikos' understanding of balance. In a place like Domotan Island, the Gods themselves have lessened sway- even the smallest of devoted cults might give ascendence to a terrible, unholy thing because of this."  </em>
	<br></p>

	<p><strong>  The Shirleighs </strong><em> - "Finally, the rulers of Domotan Island are inequivocally the Shirleighs. By birthright and by favor of their deity, the Jealous God, they hold a control over the island at the chagrin of the Elemental-worshippers that serve as their citizenry. At odds with the Katholikos, their flagrant heresy is tolerated under strict stipulations, effectively a truce between the two for the betterment of the island itself."  </em>
	<br></p>
